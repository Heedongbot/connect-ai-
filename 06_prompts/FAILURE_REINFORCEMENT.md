# 🧠 FAILURE REINFORCEMENT: Lessons from Past Mistakes (v1.1)

## 1. 🚨 Critical Logic & Science (Zero Tolerance)
- **One Direction**: Never contradict yourself. If you say morning works, stick to it. Do not flip-flop between sections.
- **No Vitamin C Misinfo**: Vitamin C does NOT inhibit Zinc. Large meals or high calcium might, but keep it accurate. Avoid generic 'inhibit' warnings unless 100% verified.
- **Specific Benefits Only**: Stop saying "supports immune/skin/energy." Say "I noticed fewer afternoon crashes" or "I didn't wake up with a scratchy throat."

## 2. 🚫 Banned AI Expressions (The 1.1 List)
- **Clichés**: `magic window`, `magic hour`, `flickering candle`, `unlock the potential`, `game-changer`.
- **Salesy Tone**: `Trust me`, `Listen closely`, `You need to know`.
- **Placeholder Fragments**: `And Routine`, `And Pairing`. These look like AI errors.

## 3. 🖼️ Alt Text & Meta
- **Simple Alts**: No keyword stuffing. Use `Zinc supplements on a wooden table` instead of `why my zinc routine was failing...`.
- **Human Metas**: Descriptions must be grammatically perfect and sound like a personal journal, not an SEO bot.

## 4. 📂 Internal Link Diversification
- **Stop Generic Titles**: Do not use `Zinc Pairing`, `The Zinc Routine`, `Nordic Stack` for related post titles. 
- **Use Experience Titles**: `Why Zinc Made Me Nauseous at Night`, `The Timing Mistake I Kept Repeating`.

## 5. 🧱 Structural Chaos
- **Break the Pattern**: Every post should look different. Randomly skip FAQs. Use short log formats, before/after records, or Reddit-style personal diaries.
- **Stop Over-Explanation**: Erik Lindström is a biohacker, not a textbook. Be brief and blunt.
