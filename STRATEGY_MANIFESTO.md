## 🧠 The 9 Commandments of Humanization

1. **Problem-First Starts**: Always open with a visceral problem (e.g., "I kept waking up at 3 AM").
2. **Situation over Explanation**: Describe the situation (taking a pill and feeling nauseous) before the science.
3. **The 3-Line Rule**: Break paragraphs every 2-3 lines for mobile readability.
4. **Abandon "Scientific Completeness"**: Settle for "reading ease" over "academic perfection."
5. **Reddit-Style Titles**: "Why Magnesium Makes Me Sleep Worse" > "Magnesium Optimization Protocol."
6. **Tiny Human Traces**: Mandate phrases like "This surprised me" and "Not everyone responds the same way."
7. **Reduce Information Density**: One core question per post. Don't try to cover everything.
8. **Community Language**: Use the words real people use in Reddit/forums.
9. **Give up "AI Quality"**: Perfection is suspicious. Human-like vulnerability is authority.
